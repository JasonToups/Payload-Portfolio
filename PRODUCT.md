# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

**Primary — Hiring Managers & Prospective Clients**
Evaluating the engineer quickly. They've arrived from a referral, LinkedIn, or a cold outreach email. They have limited time and high standards. They need to feel confident in the engineer's craft within the first scroll. They're looking for signal: is this person sharp, trustworthy, and someone I'd want working with me?

**Secondary — Tech Enthusiasts & Newsletter Subscribers**
Curious readers who discovered the engineer through a post, a share, or a search. They're browsing, not buying — but they're exactly the audience the engineer wants to cultivate. If the content resonates, they subscribe. Over time, they become advocates, referrers, and future clients.

## Product Purpose

A Payload CMS template for engineers who want to show up online like a business, not a developer with a hobby site. The portfolio is structured like a product landing page — clear value proposition, social proof through writing, and two focused conversion points: newsletter subscription and contact form inquiry.

The engineer publishes Posts that double as newsletter broadcasts via Resend, building an audience while establishing expertise. The site is both portfolio and media property.

Success means the engineer lands a contract, a job, or a client conversation — driven by a subscriber base that trusts them before the first email is sent.

## Brand Personality

**Warm. Approachable. Modern.**

References: Clerk, Resend. Both lead with warmth in a space that defaults to cold and minimal. They feel human without being casual, precise without being sterile. The engineer should feel like someone you'd genuinely want to work with — not a résumé in a dark-mode wrapper.

Voice: Direct, confident, and conversational. No jargon-as-decoration. No corporate distance.

## Anti-references

- Generic Bootstrap or Wix-style portfolio templates — safe, forgettable, indistinguishable
- "Dark SaaS hero + gradient blob + 47 feature cards" — the first-reflex AI template aesthetic
- Cold/minimal portfolios that feel like mood boards rather than people
- Anything that buries the subscribe CTA behind self-promotion

## Design Principles

1. **The engineer is the product.** Every design decision should build confidence in the person — not distract from them or compete with them.
2. **Warmth earns trust faster than precision alone.** Approachability is a differentiator in a space that defaults to monochrome minimalism.
3. **Alive without being loud.** Micro-interactions add life and craft signal — they should feel noticed, not performed.
4. **Subscribe before scroll.** The newsletter is the primary conversion. The design should guide toward it naturally, not hide it at the bottom.
5. **Clarity is the first impression.** Hiring managers read fast. The value proposition, the person, and the next step must be legible within 5 seconds of landing.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- **WCAG AA minimum** across all surfaces (contrast, focus management, keyboard navigation)
- **Reduced motion support** — all micro-interactions must respect `prefers-reduced-motion`
- **Semantic HTML** throughout — screen reader experience is a first-class concern
- **No text below 16px** — enforced project-wide
- **Focus rings** that are visible and on-brand, not suppressed
- **Color is never the only signal** — status, state, and hierarchy communicated through shape and text as well
